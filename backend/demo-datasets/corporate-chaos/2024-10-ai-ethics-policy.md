# Artificial Intelligence Ethics Policy
**Date:** 2024-10-05
**Author:** Dr. Emily Rodriguez, Chief AI Ethics Officer
**Department:** AI & Ethics

## Responsible AI Development Framework

TechCorp commits to developing and deploying AI systems that are **fair, transparent, and beneficial to society**.

## Core Principles

### Fairness and Bias Mitigation
- **Testing:** Mandatory bias testing across demographic groups before deployment
- **Diverse Data:** Training data must represent diverse populations
- **Auditing:** Quarterly audits of AI systems for discriminatory outcomes
- **Mitigation:** Documented bias mitigation strategies for all AI products
- **Accountability:** AI teams responsible for fairness outcomes

### Transparency and Explainability
- **Model Cards:** Public documentation of AI model capabilities and limitations
- **Explainability:** Users can understand how AI decisions are made
- **Disclosure:** Clear labeling when users interact with AI systems
- **Appeals:** Human review process for AI-driven decisions
- **Research:** Publish research on AI transparency methods

### Privacy and Data Protection
- **Data Minimization:** Use only necessary data for AI training
- **Consent:** Explicit consent required for AI processing of personal data
- **Anonymization:** De-identify data used in AI training where possible
- **Security:** Enhanced security for AI training datasets
- **Deletion:** Right to have personal data removed from AI systems

### Human Oversight
- **Human-in-the-Loop:** Critical decisions require human review
- **Override:** Humans can override AI recommendations
- **Monitoring:** Continuous monitoring of AI system behavior
- **Escalation:** Clear escalation path for AI errors or concerns
- **Accountability:** Humans remain accountable for AI outcomes

## Prohibited AI Applications
TechCorp will NOT develop AI for:
- Autonomous weapons or military applications
- Mass surveillance or social scoring systems
- Manipulation or deception of users
- Discriminatory targeting or exclusion
- Violating human rights or dignity

## AI Governance
- **Ethics Board:** Cross-functional AI Ethics Review Board
- **Review Process:** Mandatory ethics review for new AI projects
- **Training:** Annual AI ethics training for all AI practitioners
- **Reporting:** Quarterly ethics reports to board of directors
- **External Review:** Annual external audit of AI ethics compliance

## Continuous Improvement
- Regular review and updates to this policy
- Stay current with AI ethics research and best practices
- Engage with AI ethics community and standards bodies
- Transparent reporting of AI incidents and learnings

TechCorp believes responsible AI development is both an ethical imperative and a competitive advantage.
