# Athens Emergency Preparedness Plan
**Date:** 2023-04-15
**Department:** Emergency Management
**Author:** Emergency Management Director John Williams

## Comprehensive Emergency Response Framework

Athens' emergency preparedness plan for natural disasters, public health emergencies, and civil emergencies.

## Evacuation Procedures

### Severe Weather (Tornadoes, Hurricanes)
- **Shelter-in-Place:** Residents should shelter in basements or interior rooms
- **Assembly Points:** Six designated community shelters (schools and community centers)
- **Alert System:** Text alerts via CodeRED system (opt-in required)
- **Evacuation Routes:** Highway 78 North, Highway 316 South
- **Vulnerable Populations:** City will provide transportation to seniors and disabled

### Flooding
- **Flood Zones:** Residents in Zones A and B must evacuate immediately
- **Sandbagging:** Free sandbags available at Public Works Department
- **Water Shutoff:** Know location of main water shutoff valve
- **Insurance:** Flood insurance strongly recommended

### Extreme Heat
- **Cooling Centers:** Public libraries open as cooling centers when temp exceeds 95Â°F
- **Hydration:** Drink extra water, avoid alcohol and caffeine
- **Check on Neighbors:** Especially elderly and those without AC
- **Work Safely:** Take breaks if working outdoors

## Emergency Supplies Checklist
- 3-day supply of water (1 gallon per person per day)
- Non-perishable food for 3 days
- Flashlight and extra batteries
- First aid kit
- Battery-powered or hand-crank radio
- Prescription medications (7-day supply)
- Cash (ATMs may not work)
- Important documents in waterproof container

## Communication
- **Emergency Line:** Call 911 for life-threatening emergencies
- **Non-Emergency:** Call 311 for information
- **Updates:** Monitor local TV and radio stations
- **Social Media:** Follow @AthensEmergency on Twitter
- **Website:** www.athensga.gov/emergency (fictional)

## Community Preparedness
- Neighborhood emergency response teams
- Annual emergency preparedness fair (September)
- Free CPR and first aid training
- School-based disaster education programs

Stay informed, have a plan, and build an emergency kit!
