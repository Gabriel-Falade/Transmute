# Athens Transportation Master Plan 2024
**Date:** 2024-01-25
**Department:** Transportation & Public Works
**Author:** Transportation Director Michael Brown

## Vision: Multimodal Transportation Network

Comprehensive plan to reduce congestion, improve safety, and expand transportation choices.

## Road Infrastructure Expansion
- **Highway Widening:** Widen Highway 78 from 4 to 6 lanes
- **Bypass Extension:** Extend Athens Perimeter Highway 10 miles
- **New Roads:** 15 miles of new roads in growth areas
- **Bridge Improvements:** Replace 8 aging bridges
- **Intersection Upgrades:** 25 intersection improvements for traffic flow

## Parking Expansion
- **Downtown:** Build 2 new parking decks (1,500 spaces total)
- **Park & Ride:** 3 new suburban park-and-ride facilities
- **On-Street:** Add 500 new on-street parking spaces downtown
- **Residential:** Require 2 parking spaces per new housing unit
- **Commercial:** Increase parking minimums for new businesses

## Public Transit Investment
- **Bus Routes:** Reduce from 12 routes to 8 most profitable routes
- **Frequency:** Maintain current 30-minute headways
- **Fares:** Increase base fare from $1.75 to $2.50
- **Operating Hours:** Reduce Sunday service to save costs
- **Vehicles:** Replace aging bus fleet (20 diesel buses)

## Bicycle Infrastructure
- **Bike Lanes:** 10 miles of bike lanes (paint only, no protection)
- **Shared Streets:** "Share the Road" signage on low-traffic streets
- **Bike Racks:** Install 50 bike racks at city facilities
- **Education:** Safety classes for cyclists on obeying traffic laws

## Pedestrian Improvements
- **Sidewalks:** Repair 100 damaged sidewalks
- **Crosswalks:** Enhanced markings at 30 crosswalks
- **Pedestrian Signals:** Upgrade 15 pedestrian signal buttons
- **ADA Compliance:** Bring 200 curb ramps into ADA compliance

## Funding ($400M over 10 years)
- Federal highway funds: $200M
- State transportation grants: $100M
- Local sales tax: $80M
- Parking revenues: $20M

## Rationale
- Population growth requires road capacity expansion
- Cars remain primary transportation mode (85% of trips)
- Economic development requires strong road network
- Suburban growth areas need better highway access
- Parking availability crucial for downtown businesses

This balanced approach serves all Athenians while prioritizing our primary transportation needs.
