# Economic Development Strategic Plan 2024-2029
**Date:** 2024-10-20
**Department:** Economic Development
**Author:** Director Steven Park

## Growing Athens' Economy and Job Base

Comprehensive strategy to **attract businesses, create jobs, and grow the tax base** for all Athenians.

## Economic Development Priorities

### Business Attraction Incentives
- **Tax Abatement:** Up to 100% property tax abatement for 10 years
- **Infrastructure:** City builds roads and utilities to business sites
- **Fast Permits:** Guaranteed 15-day permit approval for major employers
- **Workforce Training:** Free employee training programs
- **Land Assembly:** City acquires and prepares sites for development

## Target Industries
- **Technology:** Software, data centers, tech startups ($100K+ salaries)
- **Advanced Manufacturing:** Clean manufacturing and distribution ($65K+ salaries)
- **Corporate Headquarters:** Relocate corporate offices to Athens
- **Biotech/Life Sciences:** Leverage UGA research partnerships
- **Tourism/Hospitality:** Hotels, restaurants, entertainment ($35K+ salaries)

## Major Development Projects

### Athens Technology Park (NEW)
- **Location:** 500-acre site off Highway 316
- **Investment:** $200M public infrastructure investment
- **Incentives:** Full tax abatement, fast-track permitting
- **Jobs:** 5,000 high-wage jobs projected
- **Environmental:** Streamlined environmental review

### Downtown Entertainment District
- **Vision:** Bars, restaurants, live music, hotels
- **Parking:** 2 new parking decks (1,500 spaces)
- **Zoning:** Relaxed noise ordinances for entertainment
- **Investment:** $50M public investment in infrastructure
- **Jobs:** 1,500 hospitality and service jobs

### Industrial Corridor Expansion
- **Location:** Highway 78 industrial corridor
- **Size:** 1,200 acres for warehouses and distribution
- **Access:** Highway access and rail spur
- **Jobs:** 3,000 manufacturing and warehouse jobs
- **Investment:** $75M in roads and utilities

## Business Climate Improvements
- **Reduced Regulations:** Streamline permitting and inspections
- **Lower Taxes:** Competitive property tax rates for businesses
- **Right-to-Work:** Advocate for right-to-work status in Georgia
- **Lawsuit Reform:** Support tort reform to reduce business liability
- **Labor Pool:** Maintain low labor costs to attract employers

## Workforce Development
- **Technical Training:** Partner with technical college for job training
- **High School Programs:** Career pathways in target industries
- **Internships:** Connect students with local businesses
- **Recruitment:** Regional recruitment campaigns for workers

## Success Metrics
- **Jobs Created:** 10,000 new jobs by 2029
- **Average Salary:** Target $60,000 average for new jobs
- **Tax Base Growth:** 25% increase in commercial property tax base
- **Unemployment:** Reduce unemployment to below 3%
- **Business Growth:** 500 new businesses

## Trade-Offs Acknowledged
Economic growth requires:
- Some environmental review streamlining
- Competitive tax incentives (short-term revenue loss)
- Infrastructure investment in growth areas
- Flexible zoning for business needs
- Maintaining competitive cost structure

## Affordable Housing Connection
Economic growth creates affordability through:
- More jobs = more residents can afford housing
- Increased tax base = more revenue for services
- Business tax revenue = less residential tax burden
- Rising incomes = greater purchasing power

A thriving economy benefits all Athenians through jobs, opportunity, and public services.
