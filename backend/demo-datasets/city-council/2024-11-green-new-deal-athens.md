# Athens Green New Deal Resolution
**Date:** 2024-11-25
**Department:** City Council
**Author:** City Council Resolution (7-3 vote)

## Athens Green New Deal: Jobs, Justice, and Climate Action

Historic city council resolution committing Athens to a **Green New Deal** approach linking climate action with economic and racial justice.

## Resolution Principles

### Climate Justice
- Climate change disproportionately harms low-income communities and communities of color
- Solutions must center environmental justice and equity
- Frontline communities must lead the transition
- Repair historical environmental injustices
- Ensure clean air, water, and green space for all neighborhoods

### Economic Justice
- Climate action creates good-paying union jobs
- Just transition for workers in fossil fuel industries
- Local hiring requirements for climate projects
- Living wages ($20/hour minimum) for all green jobs
- Worker ownership and cooperative models

### Racial Justice
- Address racist legacy of redlining and environmental racism
- Prioritize investments in historically excluded neighborhoods
- Diverse leadership in climate decision-making
- Reparative justice through targeted investments
- Community wealth building, not extraction

## Commitments

### Accelerated Climate Action (Reverses May cuts)
- **Carbon Neutral:** 2028 target (2 years faster than original goal)
- **Renewable Energy:** 100% by 2026
- **Funding:** $350M over 7 years (increased from original $250M)
- **Green Jobs:** 2,000 union jobs in climate sector by 2028

### Transportation Transformation
- **Free Public Transit:** Eliminate all bus fares (funded by progressive taxation)
- **Bus Rapid Transit:** 3 BRT lines by 2027
- **Bike Network:** 100 miles of protected bike lanes by 2028
- **Pedestrian Priority:** Eliminate parking minimums, prioritize sidewalks
- **Car-Free Zones:** Downtown car-free by 2027 except deliveries

### Housing as a Human Right
- **Public Housing:** Build 1,000 units of public housing (government-owned)
- **Rent Control:** Strict rent control caps at 3% annual increases
- **Tenant Protections:** Just cause eviction, right to counsel
- **Vacant Property Tax:** Punitive tax on vacant speculation
- **Community Land Trust:** 500 units in community ownership

### Energy Democracy
- **Municipal Utility:** Create city-owned renewable energy utility
- **Rooftop Solar:** Free solar panels for all low-income households
- **Community Solar:** Shared solar for renters and multifamily
- **Energy Co-ops:** Support community energy cooperatives
- **End Gas:** Ban all natural gas infrastructure citywide by 2026

### Green Economy
- **Worker Cooperatives:** Support and fund worker-owned businesses
- **Community Benefits:** All incentives tied to union jobs and local hiring
- **Buy Local:** Prioritize local businesses in procurement
- **Cancel Bad Deals:** Review and cancel harmful economic development incentives
- **Reparations Fund:** $10M fund for Black and Indigenous-led green businesses

## Funding Mechanisms
- **Progressive Taxation:** Higher property taxes on luxury properties ($1M+)
- **Corporate Contribution:** Require large employers to fund climate transition
- **Fossil Fuel Tax:** Tax on fossil fuel companies operating in Athens
- **Wealth Tax:** Annual tax on property values over $5M
- **Federal Grants:** Aggressive pursuit of federal climate funding
- **Green Bonds:** $200M in municipal green bonds

## Reverses Previous Policies
**EXPLICIT REVERSALS:**
- May 2024 climate budget cuts - REVERSED
- January 2024 highway expansion - CANCELLED
- March 2024 developer giveaways - REPEALED
- October 2024 technology park incentives - UNDER REVIEW

## Implementation
- **Green New Deal Office:** New city department with 25 staff
- **Community Oversight:** Community board with binding authority
- **Annual Progress:** Mandatory annual progress reports
- **Enforcement:** Penalties for city departments missing targets
- **Public Input:** Quarterly town halls in all neighborhoods

## Opposition Statement
Business groups and developers oppose these policies as "radical" and "job-killing."

We reject this false choice. **Climate action IS economic opportunity. Justice IS prosperity.**

## Historic Vote
After months of community organizing and testimony from hundreds of residents, Athens City Council voted 7-3 to adopt this resolution.

Athens chooses a future of justice, sustainability, and shared prosperity.
